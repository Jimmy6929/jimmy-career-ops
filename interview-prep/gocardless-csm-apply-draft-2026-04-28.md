At Amodeling, my AI SaaS that hit #8 on Product Hunt, I personally owned presales, onboarding, and QA. During launch week, some early users hit broken outputs and went silent. Instead of waiting for them to come back, I sent personalised re-onboarding emails to every lapsed user with a specific fix path. We recovered roughly 30% of silent-churn users — that taught me CS is engineering velocity in disguise, not just empathy.

### "Describe a time you managed conflicting priorities."
At Greener Robotics, our hardware partner shipped late and the software team was blocked on real sensor data. Instead of waiting, I restructured into parallel workstreams: ran the software against simulated sensor feeds while keeping the hardware partner on a weekly cadence. When real data arrived we merged cleanly. Result: zero schedule slip on the demo, secured a place on the STAC accelerator (£60K). The lesson: parallel beats sequential when you're under pressure — same principle applies to running an escalation in parallel with a renewal conversation.
