### Q1: Why Elyos, and why this role?

That's a higher stakes bar than most AI products. (2) **Series A $13M, YC S23, 10-ish people, 5 days in-office at Old Street** — textbook "earn and learn" stage. (3) **The CS Lead scope is the shape of work I've done as a founder across three startups** — onboarding, adoption, retention, trust in mission-critical automation. Elyos is the first Series A where that muscle obviously compounds instead of being re-learned.

### Q2: Tell us about a customer onboarding you're proud of.
> **Molebie AI's CLI onboarding wizard.** Original setup took customers hours — 5 LLM backends, env vars, API keys scattered across three places. I wrote a CLI wizard that detected the environment, prompted for credentials interactively, set sane defaults, and got users to first query in under 5 minutes. Drop-off halved within a week. The reflection: **the best onboarding documents itself.** Every recurring live question is a missing default or a missing doc, not a missing support function. For an Elyos customer whose plumbing business depends on the voice agent answering correctly on call one, the window between signed deal and first successful inbound call is the entire game. I'd build Elyos's onboarding around that — fastest path to one real successful call, documented so the next customer inherits the path.

### Q3: Tell us about a time you helped a customer trust mission-critical automation.
> **Greener Robotics, 18-month pilots with 8 farmers.** Each farmer was entrusting their daily go/no-go decision — *"do I walk the field today, or trust the IoT dashboard?"* — to automation we'd built. Trust wasn't earned in the sales pitch; it was earned across the first ten successful interactions. My playbook ran like: (1) ship the dashboard with conservative alerting so false positives were the dominant failure mode, not false negatives — farmers forgive "you told me to check but it was fine" much more than "you said it was fine and it wasn't"; (2) debrief every edge-case failure within 24 hours with the specific farmer, show what we changed, name who's accountable; (3) use the customer's language, not ours — "the app," not "the IoT layer." That's the trust-in-automation playbook I'd bring into Elyos's first 30 customers.

### Q4: How would you define adoption and retention for an Elyos SMB customer?
> Adoption = **weekly call volume being handled by the agent without a human escalation trigger, against their pre-signup baseline.** If the plumbing SMB was taking 30 inbound calls/week pre-Elyos and the agent handles 27 of them end-to-end in month two, we have adoption. Retention = **that number staying stable or growing in month four, with the customer defaulting to "let the agent try first" on new use cases.** The opposite of adoption is silent drop-off — the customer stops routing calls to the agent but doesn't cancel. That's what the CS function exists to catch in week six before it becomes a churn call in month four. The first CS hire builds the dashboard / review cadence / intervention playbook that makes silent drop-off impossible.
