### Q2: Tell us about a time you built or grew a community.

> As **Marketing Director at Glasgow University Entrepreneurship Society (Jan 2022 - May 2023) I grew paid membership from 45 to 150+ in one year - 233% YoY.** The full playbook, because the numerator matters less than the inputs:
>
> - **Cadence:** Weekly high-signal event + monthly flagship. The weekly kept the community showing up; the flagship (pitch night, founder panel) was the acquisition engine.
> - **Formats that worked:** Founder-panels with actual local founders doing Q&A, not generic "how to start a startup" talks. Pitch-practice nights where members pitched each other for 3 minutes and got real feedback. Both worked because members left with something specific they couldn't get on YouTube.
> - **What killed engagement:** Generic networking mixers with no agenda. We killed them after two attempts. Lesson: community members need a reason to show up that isn't just "other people will be there."
> - **Retention mechanism:** Shifted from one-off attendance to a lightweight membership identity - branded swag, a members-only Slack/mailing list, and a rolling "help-a-founder" board where active members solved each other's problems. The active-members flywheel is what moved 45 to 150+, not top-of-funnel marketing.
> - **Reporting:** Monthly numerator review with the committee - new signups, active members, event NPS proxy (show-up rate + repeat rate). Closed the loop on every format.
>
> Same muscle I'd run against Primigos: cadence, format discipline, a real retention mechanism, and a numerator I actually trust.

### Q4: How would you measure success in the first 6 months at Primer?

> First-principles numerator: **developers who ship their first Primer integration after attending a community event or touching the Primigos channels.** That's the one metric that connects community work to revenue - everything else is proxy.
>
> Dashboard around that:
> - **Engagement depth, not reach:** DAU/WAU in Primigos channels (Slack/Discord/wherever it lives), and what % of monthly actives post or reply at least once. Reach without depth is vanity.
> - **Event NPS + repeat-attend rate.** NPS alone is noisy; repeat-attend is the honest signal that the event is worth showing up to twice.
> - **Retreat / workation output:** not just "did it happen" but how many cross-team collaborations, internal docs, or product ideas trace back to an in-person moment 90 days later.
> - **Ambassador / advocate count:** Primigos members who bring in other merchants or devs without being asked. That's where community becomes distribution.
>
> First 6 months I'd expect to (1) audit what's already working in Primigos and kill what isn't, (2) ship one flagship event format and iterate it twice, (3) stand up the measurement dashboard end-to-end so the second half of year one is data-driven, not vibes-driven.
