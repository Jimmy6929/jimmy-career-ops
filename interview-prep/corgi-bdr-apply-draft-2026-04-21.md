### Q2: Tell us about a time you built pipeline from nothing.
> At **Greener Robotics** I cold-emailed 40+ investors to land live 15-minute demo slots at **Barclays Eagle Labs, the MIT Climate & Energy Prize, and YC Startup School** — three high-signal rooms in four months. The motion was recognisably BDR: ICP filtering (investors whose portfolio had a climate/IoT thesis), cold outbound with a specific value hook per investor, follow-up cadence, qualified meetings booked. The key reflection: **cold outreach converts on specificity + brevity, not charm.** A founder name-dropped in the second sentence + a one-sentence thesis on why their portfolio mattered to us + a single ask (15 minutes) won more meetings than any longer email I tried. That's the same muscle your BDRs run against cyber-insurance / D&O prospect lists every day.

### Q3: Tell us about a time you followed someone else's playbook, even when your instinct was to change it.
> At **Amodeling** (our AI SaaS) we borrowed the **Product Hunt launch playbook** end-to-end from a YC-alum friend who'd hit #1. My builder instinct was to improvise — rewrite the copy, tweak the launch time, skip the pre-launch hunter outreach. Didn't do that. Followed the playbook exactly: mid-day-UK launch (US morning), pre-launched to 20 hunters 48 hours prior, ran a teaser thread 36 hours out, comment-swarmed hour 1 and hour 6, DM'd everyone who upvoted within 24 hours. Result: **#8 on Product Hunt, 106 upvotes on launch day.** The reflection I carry: *the playbook is what wins until you've run it enough to know which piece to improvise on.* That's exactly the discipline I want to apply to Corgi's US-proven sales motion for the first 12 months.
